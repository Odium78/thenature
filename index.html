Effects and Impacts of SDG 17 on the Tech Industry

Driving Technology Transfer and Innovation

SDG 17 emphasizes global cooperation in science, technology, and innovation (United Nations, 2015).
This encourages technology companies in developed economies to share innovations with developing nations.
For instance, the UN Technology Bank helps least developed countries access scientific knowledge
and digital tools (United Nations, 2024b). This transfer accelerates innovation
in fields like renewable energy, health technology, and artificial intelligence.

Expanding Digital Infrastructure and Connectivity

One of the key targets (17.8) focuses on enhancing ICT access. Global internet penetration
reached 68% in 2024, but 2.6 billion people remain offline (United Nations, 2024a).
This presents both a challenge and an opportunity for the tech industry. Companies like telecom providers,
cloud platforms, and device manufacturers benefit from demand for affordable and widespread connectivity solutions.
